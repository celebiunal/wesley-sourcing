Why Work With a Local Yiwu Sourcing Agent?

Yiwu gives buyers access to a huge supplier base across consumer goods, retail products, hotel supplies, accessories, packaging, home goods, and many small-MOQ categories. A local sourcing agent can compare suppliers in person, clarify specifications in Chinese, and reduce the risk of miscommunication.

China Supplier Verification Process

We help verify whether a supplier is a realistic match before you place an order. This includes checking business details, product experience, sample quality, quotation consistency, communication reliability, and whether the supplier understands your target market requirements.

Quality Control Before Shipment

Quality problems are easier to solve while goods are still in China. We coordinate sample checks, production follow-up, pre-shipment inspection, photo or video documentation, and corrective communication with the supplier before shipping is arranged.

Small MOQ Sourcing From China

Many European SMEs and e-commerce sellers cannot start with full-container orders. We look for suppliers and product options that fit practical starting quantities, then help consolidate items from multiple sources when that is the most efficient path.

Common Risks When Buying Directly From Chinese Factories

Common risks include unclear specifications, trading companies presented as factories, inconsistent samples, late production updates, packaging mistakes, quality issues found after shipment, and expensive logistics decisions. Local follow-up reduces these risks before they affect your customer.

Landmark Case

Silvain v. Ye Yongqing — China's Highest Art Copyright Award

In what became the most significant art copyright case in Chinese legal history, Wesley Sourcing led the full process of defending Belgian artist Christian Silvain against Chinese painter Ye Yongqing, who had copied 122 of Silvain's works over nearly 30 years.

Our team identified the infringement, assembled the legal team, coordinated evidence collection across two continents, and managed the entire litigation process. The artist himself provided only the foundational evidence — we did the rest.

In 2023, the Beijing Intellectual Property Court ruled in Silvain's favor. Following appeal, the Beijing High Court upheld the decision in January 2025. Ye Yongqing was ordered to pay RMB 5 million (approx. €650,000) in damages and issue a public apology — the highest compensation ever awarded in a Chinese art copyright case.

China Sourcing FAQ

Direct answers for European buyers comparing China sourcing agents, supplier verification, quality control, and logistics support.

What does a China sourcing agent do?

A China sourcing agent helps buyers find suitable suppliers, compare pricing, verify factories, manage samples, follow production, inspect goods, consolidate orders, and coordinate shipping. The goal is to reduce risk and save time when buying from China.

Why use a sourcing agent in Yiwu?

Yiwu is one of the world's largest wholesale markets and is useful for product discovery, small-MOQ sourcing, and multi-category buying. A local Yiwu sourcing agent can visit suppliers, compare options, and communicate directly in Chinese.

Can you help with small orders?

Yes. Wesley Sourcing helps SMEs, retailers, and e-commerce sellers find realistic supplier options for smaller starting quantities. When needed, we can coordinate multiple suppliers and consolidate products before shipment.

How do you verify suppliers in China?

We review supplier details, product experience, communication reliability, sample quality, quotation consistency, and production capability. For suitable projects, we can also arrange local checks or visits before larger commitments.

Do you provide quality inspections?

Yes. We can support sample checks, production follow-up, pre-shipment inspection, packaging checks, photo or video reporting, and issue resolution with suppliers before goods leave China.

Can you consolidate products before shipment?

Yes. If your order involves several suppliers, we can help coordinate delivery to a consolidation point, check quantities, combine shipments, prepare documentation, and arrange freight options based on your destination.
